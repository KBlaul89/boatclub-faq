Where can I boat in San Diego?

We currently have six convenient locations with more than 60 boats available to accommodate all types of boaters.

  • Dana Landing: Mission Bay, pontoons, deck boats, bow riders. Limited fishing vessels.
  • Cabrillo Isle: Harbor Island, fishing-focused with Key West center consoles and Defiance pilothouses.
  • Marina Cortez: Harbor Island, premium cruising vessels including dual-console bow riders.
  • Oceanside Harbor: Direct Pacific Ocean access with offshore-capable vessels.
  • Chula Vista Harbor: Relaxed cruising and South Bay access.
  • Seaforth Mission Bay: Family-friendly Mission Bay location with cruising vessels.

How often can I boat?

Frequency depends on membership type. We recommend booking at least 2 weeks in advance during summer months. Reservations can be changed or canceled. No-Show Fee: $25.

What if no boats are available when I want to book?

Add your name to the waitlist. You can request by boat type or by specific vessel name. Dock staff will contact waitlisted members in order of sign-up if cancellations occur.

How do reservation times work?

  • Morning: Start 8 AM. Return by 1 PM in summer or 12 PM in winter.
  • Afternoon: Start 2 PM in summer or 1 PM in winter.
  • All-day: Must return by sundown. Notify staff of changes to avoid fees.

What is Early Check-Out?

Members may request sunrise departures. Boats are prepped the night before and access is provided with a lockbox code. Complete the checklist and leave it in the dock box before departure.

What are the fishing rules?

Fishing vessels have bait tanks. Dump bait before returning. No fish cleaning on boat surfaces (bring a cutting board). Lobstering is not permitted. Members receive 10% off *Team Defiance* charters.

Can I fish in Mexico?

Yes, with a Golden State Boat Lease Agreement. Members are responsible for their own licenses, passports, and FMM permits.

How far offshore can I take the boat?

Up to 50 miles offshore in U.S. waters and no farther than 1 mile south of Coronado Islands in Mexico. Restrictions apply during Small Craft Advisories or Gale Warnings.

Can I swap boats?

No. Boat swapping is not permitted. Cancellations automatically notify the waitlist.

Do I tip dock staff?

Tipping is optional but appreciated. Tips are pooled among staff on duty.

What is the check-in procedure?

Call the dock phone upon arrival. Dock staff will meet you, check ID, and escort you to your vessel. A safety checklist will be reviewed before departure.

How do I return the boat?

Hail staff on VHF Channel 87 before docking. Staff will assist and charge for fuel upon check-in. If you don‚Äôt refuel, a $25 fee applies (waived if under 5 gallons).

What if authorities board my boat?

Authorities may perform safety checks. Required safety gear is in the orange box onboard. Children under 13 must wear life jackets while underway.

What if my boat breaks down?

Anchor safely, then call Sea Tow (info in orange box). Notify dock staff once tow is confirmed.

Can I beach the boat?

Only pontoons may be beached. Members must monitor tides. If retrieval is required, a $300 fee applies.

Is smoking allowed?

No. Smoking onboard results in a $150 Cleaning Fee.

Do boats have stereos?

Some boats have Bluetooth stereos, but most do not. Members should bring portable speakers.

Does FBC provide water sports gear?

Limited life jackets, tow harness, and air pump available at Dana Landing (first-come, first-served). No fishing gear provided.

Where can I buy food or bait?

  • Dana Landing: Market & deli with bait
  • Cabrillo Isle & Marina Cortez: Onsite delis
  • Bait Barge: Located south of Shelter Island
